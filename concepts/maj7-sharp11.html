maj7♯11

Lydian chord

Prerequisites Major 7th chord

A maj7 with the 11th raised by a semitone. That sharpened 11th (equivalent to a tritone above the root) is what gives this chord its particular floating, otherworldly quality.

In C: C · E · G · B · F♯. The F♯ rather than F avoids the minor 9th clash with the E that a natural 11 would create — this is why the ♯11 sounds open where the natural 11 sounds muddy.

1 · 3 · 7 · ♯11

Voicing on piano

Drop the 5th (you almost always can). A favourite voicing: root in the left hand, then stack 3rd · ♯11 · 7th in the right — the tritone between 3rd and ♯11 is the whole point, let it ring.

Character

This is the Lydian sound — bright, hovering, slightly unreal. Kiefer uses it constantly. It sounds like a question that isn't anxious about being answered.

Context

Works beautifully as a I chord when you want to avoid the IV, as a resting point in neo-soul and jazz-adjacent harmony, and anywhere you want major tonality with an added shimmer.
