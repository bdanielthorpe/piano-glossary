Major Triad

Foundation concept

The major triad is the most fundamental chord in Western harmony — three notes built by stacking a major third, then a minor third on top.

In C major: C · E · G. The interval from C to E is a major third (4 semitones), and from E to G is a minor third (3 semitones).

1 · 3 · 5

On the piano

Root position places the root at the bottom. But the triad is equally valid in first inversion (3rd in the bass) or second inversion (5th in the bass) — same notes, different colour.

Why it sounds the way it does

The major third sits close to the 5th harmonic of the root — it's baked into the overtone series. This is why the major triad sounds stable and "bright" without needing explanation. It's not a cultural convention, it's physics.

Minor 7th Chord

min7

Prerequisites Minor triad 


A minor 7th chord is a minor triad with an additional interval of a minor seventh from the root. Equivalently, we can think of the minor seventh as living a tone below the root.
