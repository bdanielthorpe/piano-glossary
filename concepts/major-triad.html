Major Triad

Foundation concept

1 · 3 · 5

The major triad is the most fundamental chord in Western harmony — built by stacking a major third, followed by a minor third.

Cmaj:

C · E · G

On the piano

Root position places the root at the bottom. But the triad is equally valid in first inversion (3rd in the bass) or second inversion (5th in the bass) — same notes, different colour.

Why it sounds the way it does

The major third sits close to the 5th harmonic of the root — it's baked into the overtone series. This is why the major triad sounds stable and "bright" without needing explanation. It's not a cultural convention, it's physics.
