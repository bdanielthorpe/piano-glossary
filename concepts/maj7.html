Major 7th Chord

maj7

Prerequisites Major triad

Take a major triad and add the note a semitone below the octave. That's the major 7th — and it transforms a stable triad into something altogether more luminous.

In C: C · E · G · B. The B wants to resolve up to C, but in the context of the chord it just hangs there, beautifully suspended.

1 · 3 · 5 · 7

Voicing on piano

The classic neo-soul voicing drops the 5th entirely — you don't need it. Root in the left hand, then 3rd, 7th, and maybe a colour tone in the right. Spread voicings (where the 7th sits above the octave) open it up even further.

Character

Dreamy, sophisticated, at rest but not boring. The maj7 is the chord that sounds like Sunday morning. It's home, but an interesting home.
